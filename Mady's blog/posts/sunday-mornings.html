Sunday Mornings & Slow Coffee

March 28, 2026  ·  3 min read

✦

Singapore mornings have a particular quality before the heat sets in. The light comes in low and golden through the kitchen window, the air is still just about bearable, and the city is doing that rare thing where it almost feels quiet. I have loved this time of day my whole life, but I love it differently now that it belongs entirely to me.

Since leaving corporate life I have started waking up early on Sundays on purpose. Not for productivity, not to get ahead of the laundry or the meal planning or any of the hundred things that fill a week. Just to sit. To make coffee slowly, hold the mug with both hands, and be in the morning before anyone else needs me to be in the day.

Mason wakes up not long after. He comes downstairs with his hair going in four directions, eyes still soft from sleep, and immediately wants to help with something. He is two years old and very serious about being useful. On the mornings he finds me already up he looks at me with a kind of satisfaction, like he knew I would be there. He climbs up beside me and we sit together for a few minutes, not really talking, just existing in the same warm space. It is one of my favourite things in the world.

Some of the most important moments in a life are the ones where nothing is happening at all.

The small ceremony

I do not have a strict morning ritual. Some Sundays I write. Some Sundays I read a few pages of whatever book I am in the middle of. Some Sundays I just sit with my coffee and think, or do not think, and let the morning do what it wants with me. The only constant is the stillness and the window and the feeling that I am choosing this, that nobody is waiting for a deliverable and nothing needs to be escalated and this hour is genuinely mine.

For fifteen years I gave my best hours to work. I am not bitter about that, it served its purpose and I am grateful for it. But there is a particular sweetness in reclaiming the morning for something that asks nothing of you except to be present in it.

By the time Meimei is up and the house is fully awake and the day has properly begun, I am ready. Not because I have been productive, but because I have been still. There is a difference, and I spent a long time not knowing it.

I hope you find your own version of this, whatever it looks like for you. The small daily ceremony that brings you back to yourself before the world takes you somewhere else. Mine is a slow Sunday coffee and a quiet Singapore morning. It is not glamorous. It is everything.

With love, Mum x
