A Letter to You, Right Now

March 8, 2026  ·  4 min read

✦

My darlings,

I want to tell you a few things today. Not because something dramatic has happened, but because these feel like truths worth writing down while they are sitting so clearly in me. I have been doing a lot of thinking lately, the kind that happens when your life changes shape and you suddenly have the time and the quiet to actually examine things properly.

You are not the words others say about you

I have spent a lot of my own life carrying things people said about me, filing them under permanent truths when really they were just passing comments from passing moments that the other person had forgotten by the following week. It took me a long time to understand that the harshest critic in my life was never anyone else. It was me, amplifying old words long after the person who said them had moved on.

Please hear this: you are not a collection of other people's opinions of you. The things that get said in your direction, whether at school or at work or in families, are filtered through that person's own life and experiences and unexamined feelings. Very little of it is actually about you. Learn to hold words lightly. Keep what is useful and let the rest go.

Time is the only currency that matters

I left my career recently, and when I try to explain why, what it comes down to is this: I finally understood that time cannot be earned back. Money can be rebuilt. Reputations can be rebuilt. But an hour spent in the wrong place, doing the wrong thing, for the wrong reasons, is gone permanently. I did not want to spend any more of my hours that way.

If something costs you your health, your peace, or your integrity, it is too expensive. No matter how impressive it looks from the outside.

You will face moments when the world offers you things that look very valuable and cost you something invisible. Pay attention to the invisible cost. It is always the one that matters.

You already have what you need

When you hit a wall, and you will, resist the temptation to conclude that you are missing a resource. In almost every case, what you are actually missing is the resourcefulness. The creativity, the determination, the willingness to care enough to find another way. These things are inside you already. They always have been. Trust them.

I am so proud of you

Not for what you achieve. For who you are. Mason, I watch you move through the world with such quiet depth and I am in awe of you. Meimei, I watch you fill every room you enter with warmth and I am in awe of you too. You are both so entirely, specifically yourselves, and I would not change a single thing.

Whatever age you are when you read this, whatever life looks like for you right now: I hope you know you are deeply loved. Not because of what you have done or become. Just because you exist, and you are mine, and I am so unspeakably lucky to be your mother.

All my love, always,

Mum x
