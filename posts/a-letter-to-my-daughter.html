To My Girl Who Is Already So Much

March 25, 2026  ·  4 min read

✦

My Meimei, you are less than a year old and I can already see it in you: the way you watch everything, the way you respond to the people around you, the way your whole face changes when someone you love walks into the room. You are already so full of joy and warmth and a kind of bright, buzzing energy that makes every room feel more alive.

But I also see something else, something I recognise because I have lived it myself. Even now, so young, I can see the beginning of the wanting-to-get-it-right. The watching to see if the response is good. The sensitivity to whether you have pleased the people you love. My darling, I see you, and I want to get ahead of something before you carry it for longer than you need to.

You do not have to earn your place here

You have had your place in this family since the moment you arrived, before you could do a single thing. Before you could smile or sit up or reach for us or make us laugh. You were already completely, unconditionally ours, and that has never once been conditional on your performance.

I want you to grow up knowing that my love for you is not a reward. It is not something you can lose by being imperfect, by being difficult, by getting things wrong, by falling apart on a hard day. It is just there, constant and enormous and entirely independent of what you achieve or how you behave or whether you have been good enough by anyone else's measure.

You are loved for being. Not for doing. You always have been, and you always will be.

The girl I see you becoming

You are going to be a leader. Not because I want you to be, but because I can already see the energy in you that draws people in, that wants to organise and connect and make things happen. You are going to be the one people look to. That is a gift, and it comes with its own kind of weight, and I am going to spend your whole childhood making sure you know the difference between leading because you want to and performing because you think you have to.

I want you to dance. I want you to do drama and make art and get clay on your hands and be as messy and loud and wildly yourself as you can possibly be, not to develop skills, but because I need you to know in your bones that you are loved when you are imperfect. That the untidy, unpolished, fully honest version of you is the one I love most of all.

What I am here to be for you

I am your fence. Not to keep you in, but to make sure that no matter how far you roam or how big the world gets, you know exactly where the safe place is. I am the voice that says: come back here, you are enough, you do not have to be anything other than what you already are.

You are a garden, my love. You do not need to pull your own weeds. That is what I am here for. Just grow.

All my love, always, Mum x

✿ ✦ ✿ Made with love, for my two favourite people in the world.
