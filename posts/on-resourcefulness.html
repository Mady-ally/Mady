What I Really Want You to Understand About Getting What You Want

March 15, 2026  ·  4 min read

✦

Somewhere along the way, most of us are taught to believe that not having enough is the reason we cannot get what we want. Not enough money. Not enough time. Not the right connections or the right background or the right circumstances. We learn to look outward for the thing that is missing, and when we cannot find it we make it mean that we cannot go further.

I have believed this at different points in my life, and I have watched the people I admire most prove it wrong, not by having more, but by thinking differently about what they already had.

Resources are not the problem

I once heard an idea that I have turned over in my mind ever since. When people fail to reach something they genuinely wanted, they will almost always tell you they were lacking the resources: the money, the time, the support, the opportunity. But look more closely, and what you almost always find is that they were lacking the resourcefulness. The willingness to be creative, to be determined, to care deeply enough about the outcome to find a way when the obvious way was blocked.

Creativity is a resource. Determination is a resource. Love, flexibility, courage, the refusal to accept the first no as the final answer: all of these are resources. And unlike money or connections or perfect timing, these are not things that have to be found outside yourself. They are already in you.

If you are creative enough, you will find the idea. If you are committed enough, you will find the capital. If you care deeply enough about other people, you will find the people who will help you. The resources follow the resourcefulness.

What this looked like in my own life

There have been several moments in my life where I genuinely had very little. Moments where the practical resources were thin and the situation felt stuck. What got me through every single one of them was not a sudden windfall or a lucky break, though occasionally those came too. It was the decision, made quietly and sometimes with trembling hands, to be resourceful instead of helpless. To ask a different question. To find a way instead of cataloguing the ways it could not work.

I am not always good at this. There are days I slip back into the old story: I do not have enough, I am not enough, the conditions are not right. But I have lived long enough now to know that those are feelings, not facts. And feelings can be examined and, with practice, redirected.

What I hope for you

I do not want you to grow up believing that your circumstances determine your ceiling. Singapore is a small island, but we are surrounded by people who started from nothing and built remarkable lives precisely because they could not afford to be anything other than resourceful. That spirit is in your blood and in this city.

I want you to fall in love with solving problems. To see a closed door and feel curious, not defeated. To understand that the most important tools you will ever need are not things you can buy, and they cannot be taken from you either. They live in how you think, how much you care, and how willing you are to keep going.

That is the kind of success I am rooting for you to find. Not the kind measured in titles or money, though those things have their place. The kind measured in the look on your face when you solved something that seemed impossible. That look, I promise you, is available to you every single day.

With love, Mum x
