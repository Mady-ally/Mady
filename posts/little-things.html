The Little Things I Never Want to Forget

March 20, 2026  ·  4 min read

✦

They say you will always remember. But the truth is, you will not, not all of it. The small and ordinary details of a life have a way of fading, replaced by other small and ordinary details. So I am writing this down right now, while it is vivid and real and happening all around me every single day.

These are the little things I never want to forget.

Mason and new words

You have always had an extraordinary relationship with language. You file new words away somewhere private and then produce them later, days after you heard them, in exactly the right context, with the casual confidence of someone who has always known them. The look on your face when a word lands perfectly, when you can see that I understood exactly what you meant, is one of the purest expressions of joy I have ever witnessed. I never want to forget that look.

Meimei's laugh

Your sister laughs with her whole body. There is no half measure with her. When something is funny, she commits to it completely, this full-throated, joyful sound that fills whatever room she is in and makes everyone nearby involuntarily smile. I have never been able to hear that laugh and remain in a bad mood. It is physically impossible. I hope she never loses it.

The way you both reach for me

Mason, when we are out walking, crossing the road, moving through a crowd, your hand finds mine without a word. Not because you are afraid. Just because I am there and you want the connection. I hold that hand carefully every single time, knowing it will not always be offered so freely. It is the greatest honour of my ordinary days.

The little things are not little at all. They are the whole thing, and I know it even as they are happening, which makes them even more precious.

The questions at the end of the day

Mason, you save your most interesting thoughts for bedtime. The moment I have tucked you in and am reaching for the light: Why do cars have wheels and not legs? Where does the sky end? Will we always live in Singapore? I used to gently redirect you towards sleep. Now I sit on the edge of the bed and we talk, properly, for as long as you need. I would rather be tired tomorrow than miss a single one of these conversations.

The everyday Singapore of it all

The morning walks with Papa to see the cars and the road. Breakfast at the coffee shop with kaya toast and the particular way Singapore sounds at 8am. Reading before meals, every meal, until it became as natural as eating itself. The sand and the sun and the two of you in the morning light of this city that I love and that is the only home either of you has ever known. I want you to know it through my eyes too, one day. I hope these pages do that.

These are the little things. And they are everything.

With all my love, Mum x
