On Running a Family With Intention

March 22, 2026  ·  4 min read

✦

We run our businesses with guiding principles and mission statements and north stars. We build entire systems around making sure teams are aligned, values are clear, and everyone knows what the culture is and where it is going. And then we come home and let the most important institution in our lives run entirely on autopilot.

I have been thinking about this a lot since leaving corporate life. Now that I am home, fully home, I feel the weight of the opportunity. Not just to be present for the daily moments, but to be intentional about the kind of family we are building. What do we stand for? What do we do when things are hard? What do we pass down?

The family I want us to be

I want our family to be a place where the truth is always safe. Where you can tell me the hard thing, the embarrassing thing, the thing you are not sure about, and find only curiosity and love on the other end. I want our home to be the place you run towards when life gets difficult, not away from.

I want us to be a family that reads together. That eats dinner together without phones, talking about the day and the ideas and the small observations that do not seem important until years later when you realise they were everything. I want the rituals, the small repeated ceremonies of a life lived together, to become the things you carry with you when you go.

The family we are is not something that happens to us. It is something we build, deliberately, one ordinary evening at a time.

The values I want to live out loud

Family love comes first. We accept each other as we are, and we are never afraid to say when something feels different or hard or wrong. We tell each other the truth because we respect each other too much not to. We are each other's safe harbour, which means the harbour is always open, unconditionally.

We do not fear failure in this family. We treat every mistake as information, as data about what to try differently next time. We are not fragile. We are a family that gets stronger when things get difficult, because we face the difficult things together instead of pretending they are not happening.

We give back. However we are able to, in whatever form makes sense in the season we are in, we look for ways to add something to the world rather than just taking from it. This matters to me deeply, and I hope it becomes something you choose for yourselves too, not because I told you to, but because you have seen what it does to a person when they live this way.

Why I am writing this down

Because most families exist only in the nebulous present. No written history and no written future. When a crisis hits, there is nothing to return to. I want you to have something to return to. Not rules, not a performance review of whether you have been a good enough family member. Just a reminder of who we are and what we stand for when the world gets loud and confusing.

You belong to something here. Something that was built with enormous care and love and intention. I hope you always feel that.

With all my love, Mum x
