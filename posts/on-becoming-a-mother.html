On Becoming a Mother

March 10, 2026  ·  5 min read

✦

I yearned to be a mother for a long time before I became one. I want you to know that, both of you. Your arrival into my life was not something that simply happened to me. It was something I had wanted with a quiet and persistent hope for years, through a long career and a complicated path and a life that had to do a great deal of rearranging before it could make room for you.

And then you came, and I realised I had not actually known what I was hoping for. I had imagined something, but the reality of it was so much larger and stranger and more overwhelming than anything I had pictured that it rendered all my preparation entirely beside the point.

The slow unfolding

Nobody tells you that becoming a mother does not happen in a single moment. I thought it would, some great immediate transformation, a switch flipped. And something does shift the instant you hold your child for the first time, something enormous and permanent. But the becoming is a slower thing. It happens across a thousand ordinary days, in the night feeds and the first words and the scraped knees and the bedtime questions that go on far longer than bedtime should.

It happened for me the first time Mason looked at me like I was the most important person in any room. The first time I stayed up all night just so he would not have to be uncomfortable alone, and discovered a patience in myself I genuinely did not know was there. It happened again when Meimei arrived, and I found out that love does not divide, it expands, and there was suddenly more of me than there had been before.

I did not become a mother the day you were born. I became a mother slowly, every single day after, in the choosing of you over and over again.

What I chose to give up, and why it was not a sacrifice

I left fifteen years of corporate life to be home with you both. People sometimes say it in a way that implies I gave something up, and I want to be honest with you about this: I left because I wanted to. Not to be a martyr. Not out of obligation. Because I had tasted what full presence felt like during maternity leave, and I could not go back to a life that asked me to be somewhere else for the best hours of the day.

I existed before you, fully and completely as my own person. I had a career that mattered to me and a life that was entirely mine. I tell you this because I want you to understand that choosing you was always a choice made with my whole self, with eyes open, with full knowledge of what it meant. You were never an obligation. You were always, always a yes.

The woman I am becoming

Motherhood has made me more honest with myself than anything else in my life. It has surfaced things I needed to look at, patterns I needed to break, ways of being that I had carried from my own childhood that I do not want to pass on to you. I am not a perfect mother. I am someone who is working at it every day, learning just as much from you as you are learning from me, trying to give you a version of love that is as unconditional as I know how to make it.

What I hope you feel, when you read this one day, is not gratitude for any sacrifice. Just the simple truth: that you were wanted, that you are loved completely, and that this ordinary life we are building together in Singapore is the best thing I have ever been part of.

With every piece of me, Mum x
